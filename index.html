Tigrinya Words and phrases

Phrases

These are common phrases you can use to greet others, ask questions, or reply. Here are some links to find more,

  • Useful Phrases
  • Tigrinya Phrasebook
  • Languages of Eritrea

Greetings

Selam = Hello/Peace
Kemey Ale'ha = How are you
Kemey Ale'hi = How are you(women)
Kemey Ale'hum = How are you(group of men/group of both genders/elderly man)
Kemey Ale'hin = How are you(group of women/elderly woman)
Kemey Hadirka = Goodmorning(male)
Kemey Hadirki = Goodmorning(women)
Kemey Hadirkum = (group of men/group of both genders/elderly man)
Kemey Hadirkin = (group of women/elderly woman)
Buroh Leyti = Goodnight
Kemey Wu'elka = Good afternoon(men)
Kemey Wu'elki = Good afternoon(women)
Kemey Wu'elkum = Good afternoon(group of men/group of both genders/elderly man)
Kemey Wu'elkn = Good afternoon(group of women/elderly woman)
Kemey Amsika = Good evening(men)
Kemey Amsiki = Good evening(women)
Kemey Amsikum = Good evening(group of men/group of both genders/elderly man)
Kemey Amsikn = Good evening(group of women/elderly woman)

Questions

Dhan Diha = Are you ok(men)
Dhan Dihi = Are you ok(women)
Dhan Dihum = Are you ok(group of men/group of both genders/elderly man)
Dhan Dihin = Are you ok(group of women/elderly woman)
Entye Malet = What does that mean
Kithegzeni thel do = Can you help me(men)
Kithegzni theli do = Can you help me(women)
Men Shimka = What's your name(men)
Men Shimki = What's your name(women)
Men Shimkum = What's your name(group of men/group of both genders/elderly man)
Men Shimken = What's your name(group of women/elderly woman)
Abey Alo ___ = Where is ___

Words

These are some words you can use to communicate when you need to say things such as stop, help, I don't know, etc. You can find more from the same links above.
